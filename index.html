Types of Kimchi!

Baechu-kimchi: (Most known type) Uses whole napa cabbage leaves and ferments them in a spicy mixture.

  1. Cut the napa cabbage into around small pieces.
  2. Add kosher or sea salt around each cabbage leaf.
  3. Place this cabbage into a large container (like a glass mason jar) and let it brine for around 6 to 12 hours.
  4. Rinse the cabbage.
  5. Mix gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), minced garlic, salted shrimp, ginger, onion, and fish sauce. You can also add radish, carrots, and green onions.
  6. Coat the paste.
  7. Pack the kimchi tightly into an airtight glass container to remove air pockets and let it ferment at a cold room temperature for 3 to 7 days.
  8. Keep the kimchi in the refrigerator!
  9. Enjoy!

Kkakdugi: A crunchy kimchi made from cubed Korean radishes.

  1. Rinse and peel Korean radishes thoroughly.
  2. Add kosher or sea salt around the radish.
  3. Let it brine for 1.5 hours to 8 hours.
  4. Drain but do not rinse.
  5. Blend garlic, ginger, gochugaru, fish sauce, small amounts of sugar, green onion, and possibly fruits like Asian pears. Mix thoroughly.
  6. Add the paste and vegetables like green onions.
  7. Pack tightly into an airtight glass container.
  8. Ferment for 1 to 3 days.
  9. Enjoy!

Dongchimi: Watery and white kimchi made from radishes. It has a clean and refreshing taste that's perfect for the summer!

  1. Wash the radishes in cold water.
  2. Add kosher or sea salt around the radish
  3. Put the salted radish into a glass jar. Put some green radish leaves and leftover salt on top.
  4. Close the lid tightly and refrigerate it for 4-5 days.
  5. Wrap ginger and garlic in cheesecloth and place them in the jar.
  6. Pour around 9 cups of water and stir the mixture with the wooden spoon.
  7. Let it sit and ferment for 2-3 days at room temperature.
  8. Enjoy!
    9. What to Eat with These Different Types of Kimchi.

    These different kimchi types are known as "banchans" in Korea, or side dishes. While it is your choice on how to eat kimchi, it is usually eaten with dishes including rice, soup, or noodles. Kimchi can also be incorporated into different soups like kimchi jjigae, kimchi fried rice, or kimchi jeon. It is the perfect side dish for any oily food as well as it balances it out with spice!
